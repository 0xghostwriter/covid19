The coronavirus (COVID-19) outbreak is changing life for all of us. You may feel anxious, stressed, worried, sad, bored, depressed, lonely or frustrated.

You’re not alone.

There are strategies for managing stress such as:

  • Limiting your intake of information from news sources about the virus
  • Maintaining social contact with friends and family by phone, text, or internet.
  • Treating your body kindly: eat healthy foods and move your body.
  • Calling your health care provider if your anxiety prevents you from being able to continue with your daily activities.
  • Learn about additional strategies to manage your stress [hyperlink to toolkit].

If you feel unsafe

Partner abuse is never okay and there are people standing by to help, especially during this health crisis. If you can, call 800-799-SAFE. If you can’t call, visit TheHotline.org to learn how to create a safety plan or for immediate help. 

The most comprehensive source for victim services programs in California is the Victims of Crime Resource Center (VCRC). The VCRC operates the confidential, toll-free 1-800-VICTIMS line and has a website.

For bilingual assistance, or if you are need of shelter, you can call the Center for Domestic Peace at 415-924-6616, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. 

If you need to talk to someone 

If you are feeling overwhelmed with sadness, depression, or anxiety, or feel like you want to harm yourself or others, you can call Disaster Distress Helpline 1-800-985-5990 or text TalkWithUs to 66746. (TTY 1-800-846-8517).

There are additional resources available:

Crisis Text Line  – Offers support 24 hours a day, 7 days a week for those in crisis. Just text: HOME to 741741

National Suicide Prevention Chat Line

Youth and Teens

  • Youth ages 12-24 can always call or text the California Youth Crisis Line, 24 hours a day, 7 days a week at: 800-843-5200.
  • Teens can talk to another teen at the TEEN LINE by texting “TEEN” to 839863 (available 6 pm to 9 pm), or by calling 800-852-8336.

Veterans

Veteran’s Crisis Line: visit the website and or call the hotline 1-800-273-8255, Press 1

First responders and law enforcement

First responders can call the Fire/EMS, helpline at 888-731-FIRE

Law enforcement personnel, call the COPLINE at 800-267-5463.

Hotline for older individuals 

Institute On Aging has a toll free line for people aged 60 years and older, and adults living with disabilities.  Knowing older adults do not contact traditional suicide prevention centers, Institute on Aging reaches out to lonely, depressed, isolated, frail and or suicidal older adults: 800-971-0016, 24 hours of the day, 7 days a week. 

Hotline for LGBTQ individuals

The Trevor Project There is information and suicide prevention resources for LGBTQ youth through the Trevor Project and you can call 866-488-7386

Hotline for Deaf and Hard of Hearing: 1-800-799-4889

For substance use disorder services, including drugs and alcohol, use the SAMHSA’s treatment locator or call the national helpline 1-800-662-HELP. You can also call your local county access line.

Helping others

Protecting children from abuse & neglect

Community members play an important role in protecting children from abuse and neglect. If you are worried about the health or safety of a child, call the local CPS hotline for the county where the child lives.

You may also be able to help children and youth who have been abused or neglected by stepping up to serve as a foster caregiver.  Please contact your local county Social Service or Human Service department (list available here: Foster Care Recruitment and Training Information), or call their toll-free at 1-800-KIDS-4-US.

Childhelp National Child Abuse Hotline: You can call 1-800-4-A-CHILD to speak with a professional crisis counselor who can provide assistance in over 170 languages. They operate 24 hours a day and also answer text and chats.  

Family Support

National Parent Helpline: Call 1-855-427-2736 Monday – Friday from 10:00am – 7pm to get emotional support from a trained Parent Advocate and become a stronger parent.

If you are concerned about a family in need of food or assistance, or you need resources yourself, call 211, contact your local non-profit Family Resource Center, or apply for public benefits, which may include health care, cash aid, and food and nutrition assistance, through your county’s Social Services or Health and Human Services department: http://www.benefitscal.org/

County Adult Protective Services

For concerns about adult abuse, neglect, or exploitation, call: 1-833-401-0832

Find mental health services

  • For general support, the California Peer-Run Warm Line offers help 24 hours a day, 7 days a week by phone or live chat.
  • If you have Medi-Cal and are in need of mental health services, call the number on your health plan membership card, or call your local county mental health line.
    • If you have Medi-Cal coverage and need help finding Medi-Cal covered services, the Medi-Cal Managed Care and Mental Health Office of the Ombudsman can help with finding the information you need: 1-888-452-8609 (Monday – Friday, 8:00am – 5:00pm)
  • California’s Aging and Adult Information Line, to connect with local services: 1-800-510-2020.
