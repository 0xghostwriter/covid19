During this coronavirus outbreak, the safest way to get medical care is by phone or online while staying at home. This is called “telehealth.”

Telehealth is the safest way to get care for symptoms of coronavirus (COVID-19).

If you have coronavirus symptoms and think you need testing or treatment, call your health care provider. Many health care providers and health plans offer telehealth options for COVID-19 screening.

If you’re having a medical emergency, you should still call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room.

Telehealth is also the best way to get most other medical care including treatment for symptoms of other conditions, mental health care, substance abuse treatment, and for follow-up visits to treat ongoing conditions during the Stay Home Order. California has relaxed certain rules so you can get care through telehealth while you’re at home.

You should not have to pay more for telehealth than you would for an in-person visit. Some health plans or providers may even waive any copay.

How to get telehealth:

  • The simplest way to get treated over the phone is to call your regular health care provider. 
  • If your doctor isn’t available, your health plan may offer other telehealth options through their member websites. If telehealth isn’t available from your doctor or your health plan, you can consult nurse advice lines.

If you have a hearing or speech disability, you can dial 711 to be connected to a free telecommunications relay service which can be used with a text telephone (TTY) or other device.  

If you have trouble accessing your regular health care provider through phone or telehealth, here’s what you can do:

For private health plans, call the state Department of Managed Health Care’s Help Center at 1-888-466-2219. 

For Medi-Cal, call the state Department of Health Care Service’s Help Center at 1-800-541-5555.
