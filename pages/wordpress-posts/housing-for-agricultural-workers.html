Housing for the Harvest is a new program that will offer temporary housing to farm and food processing workers who need to isolate due to COVID-19. It will help exposed workers protect their loved ones by giving them a space to self-isolate.  

Agricultural workers play an essential role in maintaining our food supply. But some who need to self-isolate are finding it difficult due to their housing situation. 

Under this program, the state will pay for hotel rooms for these workers who need to self-isolate. The program will provide housing for up to 14 days per participant. It will also offer support such as meals, wellness checks, and other services. Local administrators will manage the program.   

How does the program work?  

The State will:

  • publicize this program to employers and workers groups
  • contract with and secure local hotel rooms, and
  • fund this temporary housing for up to 14 days.

Local administrators will: 

  • reach out to agriculture workers who may benefit
  • serve as primary point of contact with workers who need isolation housing 
  • verify participant eligibility
  • coordinate with the state to book rooms
  • ensure services are provided in the participant’s language
  • coordinate services at the hotel site including transportation, meals and wellness checks, and
  • collect data required for FEMA reimbursement. 

Local administrators will be identified in coordination with the state government. They may be a county or city agency, tribe, non-profit organization, or a philanthropic organization.  

Who qualifies? 

Participants must meet this criteria: 

  • Work in California food processing or agriculture  
  • Meet FEMA non-congregate sheltering criteria (PDF) for COVID-19: 
    • Have tested positive, or 
    • Been exposed as documented by a public health official or medical health professional
  • Be unable to self-isolate at home

Funding and program costs

California has received FEMA approval for non-congregate sheltering during the public health crisis.

The State will seek reimbursement for hotel costs for this program at 75% federal cost share.

Local administrators will identify costs for non-housing services at the county level. Philanthropy may cover some or all costs. Some costs may be eligible for FEMA reimbursement by the county.
