Here’s what everyone can do to keep our communities healthy

Do:

  • Wash hands with soap and water for at least 20 seconds.
  • Cover your cough or sneeze with an elbow or tissue.
  • Clean and disinfect frequently touched objects and surfaces.
  • If soap and water aren’t available, use alcohol-based hand sanitizer.

Don’t:

  • Shake hands
  • Touch your face
  • Go near people who are sick
  • Go to the doctor if you aren’t sick
  • Stockpile masks or gloves

Services and information

Education

Many schools, colleges, and universities are closing temporarily or moving online.

Employment

Employees should stay home if sick. Support is available for many people who lose income due to coronavirus.

Healthcare

California is working to make testing affordable for all, and to ensure all Californians have health care coverage.

Public places

California is making recomendations to reduce the further spread of this virus.

Taxes

The state 2019 tax filing deadline is extended to June 15 for individuals and businesses unable to file on time due to COVID-19.

Get local information

Local public health authorities have the latest guidance and information.
